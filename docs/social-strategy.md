## April 1 – June 29, 2026

## The North Star

**Goal:** Establish Parker Swanson / Clockworks as the go-to trusted voice on practical AI for small business owners and professionals in the Twin Cities (and beyond). Drive consistent awareness, build an engaged audience that looks forward to your posts, and convert that audience into Cowork session clients and automation consulting leads.

## Platform Philosophy

### Facebook
Your primary conversion channel. Older professional demographic (35-60) lives here. Facebook is where trust is built over time with consistent, helpful, personal content. It's a slow burn with high payoff. The goal isn't viral — it's being the person they think of first when they finally decide to get serious about AI.

### X (Twitter)
Your authority/reach channel. This is where you build thought leadership and get discovered by journalists, peers, and the occasional viral moment. Posts here are punchier, more opinionated, and designed to attract follows from professionals who care about staying ahead.

## The Strategy: Why Everything Was Built This Way

### 1. Content Ratio: 70 / 20 / 10

- **70% Value** — Education, tips, myth-busting, real talk. This is what builds the "I always learn something from this guy" reputation.
- **20% Engagement** — Questions, polls, relatable frustrations. This is what the algorithm rewards.
- **10% Promotional** — Your services, Cowork sessions, blog posts. This stays low on purpose. The audience you build with 90% value content converts better than one you built with promotion.

### 2. Content Pillars (Rotated Weekly)

| Pillar | What It Is | Why It Works |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| 🧠 Demystify | Break down confusing AI concepts in plain English | Your audience feels left behind. You giving them "aha" moments builds loyalty |
| ⚡ Quick Win | One specific thing they can try today | Actionable content gets saved, shared, and remembered |
| 💬 Real Talk | Honest takes, cutting through the hype | 35-60 demo is allergic to BS. Honesty is your differentiator |
| 📖 Story | Real client results or your own experiences | Specificity builds credibility. Numbers matter. Names matter. |
| 🎯 Engage | Questions and conversations that invite responses | Algorithm fuel. Also shows you as human, not a content machine |
| 🔧 Tool Spotlight | Deep dive on a specific tool or feature | High search value, saves & shares |
| 📰 Blog Bridge | Tease a blog post and drive to the site | Grows blog traffic, trains the audience to look for your longer content |

### 3. The 90-Day Arc

**Month 1 (April) — Foundation**
Theme: "What AI actually is — and why most people are doing it wrong"
Goal: Voice establishment, follower growth, build trust. No hard sell.
Introduce Clockworks Cowork concept softly around Day 21.

**Month 2 (May) — Momentum**

**Month 3 (June) — Convert**
Theme: "Your AI transformation starts here"
Goal: Drive session bookings, consultation calls, email list growth
Urgency around Cowork founding rate ending. Clear CTAs introduced.

## Facebook — Detailed Best Practices Applied

### Posting Time
**Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday: 9:00 AM CST** (primary)
**Monday, Friday: 11:00 AM CST** (secondary)
**Saturday, Sunday: 10:00 AM CST** (lighter posts)

Why: Facebook engagement peaks for small business content between 9-11am weekdays when professionals are starting their day, checking their phone with coffee. Thursday 9am is consistently the highest-performing slot for professional services B2C content.

### Post Length Strategy
- **Short posts (under 80 chars before "see more"):** Hook posts, questions, quotes. The entire message is in those two lines.
- **Medium posts (150-300 words):** Story or educational posts. First two lines must create a reason to click "see more."
- **Avoid the 80-150 word dead zone:** This range gets penalized by the algorithm as "not committed to either format."

### The Link-in-First-Comment Rule
**Never post an external link in the body of a Facebook post.**
Facebook's algorithm deprioritizes posts with outbound links by approximately 50-70%. Instead:
1. Post your content normally (no link)
2. Immediately post the link as the first comment on your own post
3. Note in the post body: "Link in first comment 👇" or "Full guide in the comments"

This applies to: blog posts, Cowork page, any external URL.

### Hashtag Strategy (Facebook)
Use **3-5 hashtags per post**, placed at the very end after the post body on a new line. These hashtags are chosen for:
- Discoverability (`#AIForBusiness`, `#SmallBizAI`)
- Community (`#TwinCities`, `#MapleGrove`)
- Topic relevance (`#ChatGPT`, `#Automation`, `#Productivity`)

**Do not use hashtags mid-sentence.** Place them in a block at the end. Facebook has confirmed hashtags placed at the end perform better than inline hashtags.

**Core hashtag pool** (rotate, don't use all at once):
```
#AIForBusiness #SmallBizAI #AIAutomation #ChatGPT #ClaudeAI
#ProductivityHacks #TwinCitiesBusiness #MinnesotaBusiness
#SmallBusiness #BusinessOwner #WorkSmart #AITools
#BusinessAutomation #DigitalTransformation #n8n
```

### Emoji Strategy (Facebook)
- **1-3 emojis per post maximum**
- Place at the START of a line to replace bullets (✅ ➡️ 🔥 💡)
- Or at the END of a hook sentence to add visual pause
- NEVER use emojis mid-sentence
- Avoid generic emojis (🙂😊) — use purposeful ones (⚠️ for warnings, 💡 for insights, 🔧 for tools)

### Hook Formulas (First 2 Lines)
These are the only lines visible before "See More." They must create a reason to keep reading.

1. **The Myth-Bust:** "Most people think [X] about AI. They're wrong."
2. **The Relatable Pain:** "You've typed something into ChatGPT, got a weird answer, and closed the tab. You're not alone."
3. **The Specific Number:** "I saved a client 11 hours last week. Here's exactly what we built."
4. **The Honest Take:** "I'm going to say something unpopular about AI:"
5. **The Story Opener:** "A business owner called me at 7pm last Tuesday, panicking."
6. **The Question Hook:** "Quick question for business owners: what's the thing that takes you the most time every week that you wish someone else could do?"
7. **The Counterintuitive:** "Buying a $20/month AI subscription is not the answer."

## X (Twitter) — Detailed Best Practices Applied

### Posting Time
**Weekdays: 8:30 AM CST** (primary)
**Thursdays: 5:30 PM CST** (secondary post for threads/longer content)
**Sundays: 9:00 AM CST** (blog preview / thought of the week)

### Post Length
- **Single tweets:** 100-180 characters (shorter gets higher engagement rate per impression)
- **Thread openers:** Under 150 characters — must hook without explaining
- **Thread body tweets:** 200-260 characters — each one should be able to stand alone

### Thread Strategy
Post **2 threads per week** (one Monday, one Thursday). Format:
```
Tweet 1 (hook): One bold claim or surprising observation
Tweet 2-6 (body): Each one a standalone insight, numbered
Tweet 7 (close): CTA or landing/summary statement
```
Always **Reply to your own thread** to add the link/resource.

### Hashtag Strategy (X)
Use **0-1 hashtags per tweet**. X's current algorithm does NOT favor heavy hashtag use — it actually suppresses posts that look like hashtag-stuffing.

Rule: Only use a hashtag if it adds discovery value that the post wouldn't get otherwise.
Best: `#AITools` `#SmallBusiness` `#ProductivityTips`

### Emoji Strategy (X)
Even more restrained than Facebook. Thought leadership accounts with high engagement typically use:
- 0 emojis for serious/insight posts
- 1 emoji maximum for tips or practical posts
- Emoji at the START of a list item only

### Hook Formulas (X)
The entire tweet IS the hook on X.
1. **Counterintuitive opener:** "The best AI prompt I've ever written is 14 words."
2. **Specific observation:** "I've done 40+ AI consultations. The #1 mistake is always the same:"
3. **Thread tease:** "I spent 3 days testing every AI email tool. Here's the ranked list (thread):"
4. **Relatable frustration:** "ChatGPT giving you garbage outputs? It's not ChatGPT. It's your prompt."
5. **Bold take:** "Most AI tools are a waste of money for small businesses. The exceptions:"

### Engagement Technique (X)
- **Reply to your own posts** with follow-up context or links (this extends the conversation thread and boosts visibility)
- **Quote tweet** AI/tech news stories you disagree with or want to add nuance to
- **Reply to other creators** in the AI/small biz space — genuine, substantive replies get you seen by their audience

## Blog Strategy

### Why Tuesday (Not Sunday)

After reviewing engagement data, **Tuesday mornings** are when blog traffic peaks for business content. However, your audience of small business owners and professionals does consume longer-form content on **Sunday mornings** as "weekend reading."

**Recommendation: Publish at 7:00 AM Sunday.** This catches the Sunday morning phone scroll, gives time for your Monday morning social cross-post to catch the weekday audience, and aligns with your Cowork page launch (people plan their week on Sundays).

### Post Length Target
**1,000 – 1,400 words** per post. This is:
- Long enough to rank for long-tail keywords
- Short enough to finish in a single sitting (8-10 minute read)
- Perfectly aligned with your existing blog post format/style

### SEO Structure Per Post
```
Title: Contains primary keyword + reader benefit
Meta description: 140-155 chars, conversational, ends with a hook
H1: Same as title (or slight variation)
H2s: 3-5 section headers, each one a scannable statement
Body: Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max), no walls of text
CTA: Consistent CTA at bottom every post
```

### Writing Style Rules (Match Your Existing Post)
Based on your AI vs. Automation post, your established style is:
- Open with "Hey — I'm Parker." or a strong first-person intro
- Use real client examples (anonymized is fine)
- Short paragraphs — never more than 4 sentences
- Callout boxes for key insights
- End with a personal sign-off and soft CTA

### Repurposing Each Blog Post
Every Sunday blog post feeds the social calendar:
- **Sunday:** Blog goes live, post blog bridge tweet on X
- **Monday:** Facebook post with teaser (link in first comment)
- **Wednesday:** Pull one insight from blog as a standalone Facebook tip
- **Following week:** Pull a quote or stat as an X tweet
