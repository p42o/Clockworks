There's a night coming (late October if we're lucky, sooner if we're not) when the temperature drops through the floor and every furnace in the metro that limped through last winter decides to die at the same time. You know the one. The forecast says 18, the actual says 9, and by 6:40 a.m. your phone has gone from a quiet Tuesday to a queue.

Here's what matters about that morning: the furnaces are a hardware problem, and that's your department. But which shop the no-heat calls land at? That's mostly a software problem now, and it gets decided in the first minute of each call.

A homeowner at 58 degrees and falling isn't loyal. She isn't comparison shopping. She's calling down a list, and generally speaking, the first shop that responds like a human being gets the visit. Not the best shop. Not the cheapest. The first one that answers, or answers back. Everything below is about being that shop.

## The DIY list: what you can do this week without me

I sell systems for this, and I'll get to that. But a good chunk of cold-snap readiness is free, takes an evening, and I'd rather you do it than not. Here's the honest list.

**1. Re-record your voicemail greeting for the season.** If your greeting still says "our office hours are Monday through Friday," you're telling a freezing caller to go away politely. Record a seasonal one: what counts as an emergency, when they'll hear back, and, most important, "the fastest way to reach us tonight is to text this number." Thirty minutes, including the three takes where the dog barks.

**2. Fix your Google Business Profile before you need it.** Check the hours are real. Turn on messaging if it's off. Add a Q&A entry for "do you do emergency calls?" because people read those at 2 a.m. When the snap hits, your GBP listing is your storefront, and half the shops in the metro have a storefront that says "closed" while the owner is standing right there.

**3. Set up a basic missed-call text-back.** Many phone systems and VoIP providers can send an automatic text when a call rings out. Check yours before assuming it can't. Even a canned one helps: "Sorry we missed you. If this is a no-heat call, reply here with your address and we'll get back fast." If your carrier truly can't, keep a template in your notes app and thumb-send it to every missed number. Manual, unglamorous, works.

**4. Decide your emergency policy now, at your desk, in daylight.** After-hours rate, what you triage first, which maintenance-plan customers jump the line, what you say when you're booked out two days. Every one of those decisions is worse when you make it at 5:45 a.m. with nine voicemails and a cold truck.

Do those four and you're ahead of most of the metro. Genuinely. If that's all you take from this post, take it.
