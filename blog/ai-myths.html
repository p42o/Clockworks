The AI Myths Costing
Small Businesses Real Money

Five beliefs about AI that keep business owners stuck — and the honest truth behind each one.

Parker Swanson
CEO, Clockworks  ·  Apr 27, 2026  ·  6 min read

Hey — I'm Parker. I run Clockworks, and every week I talk to business owners who've decided AI isn't for them — usually based on something they heard that isn't true.

These aren't dumb beliefs. They made sense at some point, or they came from a real experience with a bad tool. But they're holding people back from saving real time and real money. So I want to address them directly.

Here are the five myths I hear most often — and what's actually true.

The myths

Myth 01

"You need to know how to code to use AI."

This one comes from the early days, when most AI tools required technical setup. People saw headlines about "prompt engineering" and assumed there was a steep learning curve before you could get value.

The truth

Today's AI tools — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — are conversational. You type in plain English, the same way you'd talk to a smart colleague. I've taught a 68-year-old plumber how to use Claude in 20 minutes. No code. No technical background. Just typing and reading.

Myth 02

"AI is going to replace my employees."

This fear is understandable. There's a lot of noise in the media about AI taking jobs. And honestly — some jobs will change. But the story is more nuanced than the headlines suggest.

The truth

For most small businesses, AI is a force multiplier for your people, not a replacement. An employee who uses AI for drafting, research, and admin can do the work of 1.5 people — and spend more of their time on the things that actually require a human. The businesses that will get hurt aren't the ones who adopt AI; they're the ones who don't, while their competitors do.

Myth 03

"It's too expensive for a small business."

People hear "enterprise AI" and picture a $50,000 implementation budget. Some AI deployments do cost that. Most don't — and you definitely don't need to start there.

The truth

The free tiers of ChatGPT and Claude will give you enormous value before you spend a dollar. Paid plans start at $20/month. For context: if AI saves you three hours a week and you bill at $75/hour, you've paid for a year of subscriptions in the first month. The ROI on basic AI adoption is almost always positive within the first 30 days.

Myth 04

"One AI tool does everything."

I see this go both ways: either people expect one tool to solve all their problems and get frustrated when it doesn't, or they assume they need to buy every tool on the market. Neither is right.

The truth

Different tools are genuinely better at different things. Claude is excellent for writing and long document analysis. ChatGPT has more integrations and plugins. Perplexity is great for research with cited sources. Most of my clients use two to three tools, each for specific use cases. You don't need to master all of them — just know which one to reach for based on the task.

Myth 05

"AI isn't ready yet — I'll wait until it's better."

This is the myth that costs businesses the most. The logic feels safe: "I'll wait until it matures." But this reasoning has a hidden flaw.

The truth

AI is already good enough to save you meaningful time today. Will it get better? Absolutely. But your competitors who are using it now are building workflows, habits, and experience that compounds. Waiting another year doesn't just mean you're a year behind on tools — it means you're a year behind on the institutional knowledge of how to actually use them. The best time to start was six months ago. The second best time is today.

The honest cost of these myths isn't just missed efficiency. It's the hours you spend on work AI could handle, the proposals you write more slowly than you need to, the emails you keep putting off. The opportunity cost is real — it's just invisible because you can't see the time you didn't save.

I'm not here to hype AI. I'm here to tell you the honest truth about where it actually helps and where it doesn't. But these five myths? They're not honest skepticism. They're outdated information — and holding onto them is costing you.

If any of these resonated because you've been telling yourself one of them, that's okay. The solution is just to go try something small. Pick one task. Use a free tool. See what happens.

If you're not sure where to start or want to pressure-test your specific situation, reach out. I offer free 20-minute calls and I won't push you into anything you don't need.

— Parker Swanson, CEO of Clockworks
