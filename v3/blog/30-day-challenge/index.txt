20:T24a9,<p>Hey — I&#39;m Parker. I run Clockworks, and I&#39;ve watched a lot of people try to &quot;get into AI&quot; and stall out by week two. Not because they&#39;re not capable. Because they tried to do too much at once, hit a confusing moment, and quietly stopped.</p>
<p>This challenge is designed to prevent that. One task per day. Short. Specific. Each week builds on the last. By day 30, you won&#39;t just understand AI — you&#39;ll have habits, templates, and a workflow that actually fits how you work.</p>
<p>You&#39;ll need: a free Claude account (claude.ai) or ChatGPT account, about 15–20 minutes per day, and a willingness to try something imperfect. That&#39;s it.</p>
<hr>
<h2>Week 1: Just try it — one task per day</h2>
<p>The only goal this week is to use AI for one real task every day. Not to be great at it. Not to build anything that lasts. Just to get reps in and get comfortable with the experience of asking a question and seeing what comes back.</p>
<p>Don&#39;t overthink the prompts. Don&#39;t worry if the output isn&#39;t perfect. The point is motion, not mastery.</p>
<p><strong>Week 1 — Days 1–7</strong></p>
<h3>Just try it</h3>
<p>One small task per day. Read the output. Notice what&#39;s good and what&#39;s not. No pressure to use what it gives you.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Day 1</strong> — Ask Claude to write one work email you&#39;ve been putting off. Don&#39;t send it — just read it and notice what&#39;s right.</li>
<li><strong>Day 2</strong> — Paste a long email you received and ask Claude to summarize it in 3 bullet points.</li>
<li><strong>Day 3</strong> — Ask Claude for 5 social media post ideas for your business this week. Pick the best one.</li>
<li><strong>Day 4</strong> — Write a messy paragraph about something happening in your business. Ask Claude to clean it up. Compare the two.</li>
<li><strong>Day 5</strong> — Ask Claude: &quot;I have a meeting with [describe the person or situation]. What questions should I ask?&quot; Use one of the questions in your actual meeting.</li>
<li><strong>Day 6</strong> — Ask Claude to write a short FAQ for your business — 5 questions and answers a new customer might have.</li>
<li><strong>Day 7</strong> — Look back at the week. Which task gave you the most useful output? Write that down.</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2>Week 2: Build your first template</h2>
<p>This week you&#39;re going to take the most useful thing from week one and turn it into a repeatable template. One prompt that you can use over and over with minimal changes. This is where AI starts to actually save you time instead of just being an interesting experiment.</p>
<p><strong>Week 2 — Days 8–14</strong></p>
<h3>Build your first template</h3>
<p>Pick the task from Week 1 that felt most useful. We&#39;re going to make it repeatable.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Day 8</strong> — Open a Google Doc titled &quot;My AI Prompts.&quot; Paste the best prompt from last week into it.</li>
<li><strong>Day 9</strong> — Create a Claude Project (if you haven&#39;t already). Write a 3–5 sentence description of your business and your communication style. Save it as the Project instructions.</li>
<li><strong>Day 10</strong> — Run your template prompt from Day 8 through your new Project. Notice how the output improves when Claude already knows your context.</li>
<li><strong>Day 11</strong> — Find a second recurring task — a different type of email, a report, a social post. Write a prompt for it and add it to your Google Doc.</li>
<li><strong>Day 12</strong> — Test both templates on real work from this week. Make small edits to the prompts based on what you notice.</li>
<li><strong>Day 13</strong> — Add one prompt for something you&#39;ve been meaning to do but keep avoiding. Use it today.</li>
<li><strong>Day 14</strong> — Review your Google Doc. You now have 3 working prompts. Make sure they have clear labels so you know when to use each one.</li>
</ul>
<blockquote>
<p>The Google Doc is important. Don&#39;t skip it. The people who lose their prompts have to reinvent them every time. The people who save them have a library that keeps getting better. Start building the library now.</p>
</blockquote>
<hr>
<h2>Week 3: Integrate into your daily workflow</h2>
<p>By now you have a few good prompts and you know the tools well enough to get useful output. This week is about making AI a natural part of how you work every day — not a special thing you remember to do occasionally, but a reflexive reach for a tool that&#39;s always there.</p>
<p><strong>Week 3 — Days 15–21</strong></p>
<h3>Build the daily habit</h3>
<p>This week, the goal is to use AI before you start a hard task, not after you&#39;ve already struggled with it.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Day 15</strong> — Every time you sit down to write something today, open Claude first. Before you type a word of your own, see what Claude gives you.</li>
<li><strong>Day 16</strong> — Build a template for your most common proposal type. Test it on a real one. Note what needs to be customized vs. what&#39;s reusable.</li>
<li><strong>Day 17</strong> — Use AI to prep for every meeting you have today. Even if it&#39;s just two minutes of &quot;what should I know going into this call?&quot;</li>
<li><strong>Day 18</strong> — Have a conversation with Claude about a business problem you&#39;re facing. Not asking it to write something — just thinking out loud and seeing what it reflects back. This is a different kind of use that surprises most people.</li>
<li><strong>Day 19</strong> — Build a social media content batch: ask Claude to write 7 posts for next week. Edit them to sound like you. Schedule them or save them in a doc.</li>
<li><strong>Day 20</strong> — Review your week. Where did AI save you the most time? Where did you forget to use it? Make a mental note of one situation where you&#39;ll reach for it first next week.</li>
<li><strong>Day 21</strong> — Add two more prompts to your Google Doc based on what you&#39;ve been doing this week. Your library should now have 5+ prompts.</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<h2>Week 4: Teach someone else or document what you built</h2>
<p>There&#39;s a reason this is the last week&#39;s challenge: teaching something is the fastest way to deepen your own understanding. Whether you teach a coworker, share your prompt library with someone on your team, or just write up what you&#39;ve built — the act of explaining it solidifies it.</p>
<p>Even if you work alone, documentation matters. Your future self will thank you when you come back to a prompt six weeks from now and know exactly why it&#39;s written the way it is.</p>
<p><strong>Week 4 — Days 22–30</strong></p>
<h3>Teach it, share it, document it</h3>
<p>Consolidate what you&#39;ve built and share it with at least one other person.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Day 22</strong> — Clean up your Google Doc. Add a note next to each prompt explaining when to use it and what to swap out for each situation.</li>
<li><strong>Day 23</strong> — Show someone — a coworker, employee, spouse, or friend — one of your templates. Walk them through how to use it. Notice what they find confusing.</li>
<li><strong>Day 24</strong> — Build one new workflow from scratch based on what you&#39;ve learned. Something you identified as a time sink but hadn&#39;t tackled yet.</li>
<li><strong>Day 25</strong> — Write a two-paragraph summary of what changed for you this month. What takes less time? What are you doing differently? This is for you — just honest reflection.</li>
<li><strong>Day 26</strong> — Share your prompt library with one person in your business or your network. Even if it&#39;s just emailing someone the Google Doc link with a quick note.</li>
<li><strong>Day 27</strong> — Identify the one task you still haven&#39;t figured out how to use AI for — the one that still feels manual and slow. Spend 20 minutes trying. It&#39;s okay if it doesn&#39;t work.</li>
<li><strong>Day 28</strong> — Review your Project instructions in Claude. Update them based on anything that&#39;s changed or anything you&#39;ve learned about what context actually helps.</li>
<li><strong>Day 29</strong> — Make a short list of 3 things you want to explore or improve about your AI workflow in the next 30 days. Write them in your Google Doc.</li>
<li><strong>Day 30</strong> — Celebrate. Seriously. You showed up for 30 days and built something real. That&#39;s not nothing.</li>
</ul>
<hr>
<p>At the end of 30 days you&#39;ll have a Claude Project with your business context, a library of 5–10 working prompts tailored to your specific work, at least one daily habit of reaching for AI before starting hard tasks, and a real sense of what AI can and can&#39;t do for you. That&#39;s a foundation. Everything after this is just building on it.</p>
<p>The people who do this challenge and see the most results aren&#39;t the ones who do it perfectly. They&#39;re the ones who keep going even on the days when it&#39;s annoying or the output is bad. Consistency beats perfection every time.</p>
